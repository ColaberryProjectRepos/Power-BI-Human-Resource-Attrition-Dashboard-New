Visualizations - Case 1 Objective: Reduce attrition in high-risk departments. 7
Positive Insight:

The Maintenance Department with the highest attrition rate has been pinpointed, with a concerning 64% attrition rate. This identifies a critical area where interventions can have a meaningful impact on retention.



Negative Insight:

The Maintenance Department attrition rate is resulting in $4.7M in lost productivity and replacement costs annually. This highlights the financial burden of high turnover on the organization, including expenses for recruitment, onboarding, and training of new employees, as well as the loss of expertise and productivity during transitions.



Internet Stat

Did you know the average cost to replace a terminated employee (i.e. the cost of employee turnover) is about 50 percent of that employee’s annual salary? And that number can increase based on the level of the employee and the job market in which someone is hired.

ROI:

Reducing the Maintenance Department’s attrition by 20%, the company could save $2.4M annually. This ROI reflects the savings from reduced turnover, including lower recruitment and training costs.
