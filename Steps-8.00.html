Visualizations - Case 2 Objective: Retain experienced employees. 8

Positive Insight:

Experienced employees in the Human Resource department average 12 years of service, highlighting a valuable asset to the organization.



Negative Insight:

Replacing these experienced employees is costly, with an estimated $652K in expenses associated with hiring, training, and onboarding new hires. This includes recruitment fees, the costs of training programs, lost productivity during the transition period, and the time required to bring new hires up to speed.



ROI:

Improving the Human Resource department retention by 30% could result in $541K in savings annually. These savings come from reducing the need to hire and train replacements, as well as preserving institutional knowledge that experienced employees bring.

Case 1 and Case 2 Comparison



Summary:

Both Case 1 and Case 2 offer strong opportunities for ROI: Case 1 focuses on reducing attrition in the Maintenance Department by 20%, potentially saving $2.4 million annually. This savings comes from lower turnover-related costs (e.g., recruitment and training) and the value of retaining experienced employees, which boosts productivity. Case 2 targets a 30% improvement in retention in the Human Resources department, potentially saving $541K annually. This reduction stems from fewer hiring and training expenses, as well as preserving institutional knowledge and ensuring continuity and stability that new hires cannot quickly provide.
