Executive Summary 1

What's the leading cause of Attrition in your company? In today's highly competitive business environment, employee retention plays a crucial role in a company's success. As we move further into a knowledge-driven economy, talent has become a key differentiator, making it essential to retain skilled employees. However, increasing attrition rates are making it more difficult for HR professionals to develop effective retention strategies. Attrition is no longer a minor issue but a significant concern for businesses. The solution lies in establishing strong human resource practices that address the challenges of employee retention. Acquiring a skilled workforce is only the first step; it is equally important to focus on retaining them, as losing trained and knowledgeable employees can severely impact a company's growth and market performance. This dashboard provides insights into the causes of attrition, the factors contributing to employee turnover, the challenges managers face in retaining talent and recommends strategies for improving employee retention.

Background

Employee turnover is a critical concern for businesses today, especially in industries where skilled workers are hard to replace. Predicting which employees are at risk of leaving can help HR departments proactively manage talent retention strategies. This data science project leverages historical employee data to forecast turnover, providing actionable insights that can reduce hiring costs and improve workforce stability.


Problem Statement

In today’s competitive job market, high employee turnover presents a significant and costly challenge. Gartner reports that employee turnover rates are expected to be 50–75% higher than what companies have experienced in the past, with the added complication of longer recruitment times compared to the pre-pandemic era. To address this issue, leaders must take proactive steps to equip their managers with the tools and strategies needed to retain talent while still achieving organizational goals.
