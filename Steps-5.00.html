Model Performance

The GradientBoostingClassifier model demonstrates balanced performance across several key metrics. Its Accuracy is 84.1%, indicating that all predictions (both positive and negative) were correct. The F1 score, also at 84.1%, reflects a harmonic mean of Precision and Recall, making it a reliable metric for evaluating the model, especially in imbalanced datasets. Precision stands at 84.2%, which shows that the instances predicted as positive were indeed correct. Recall is also 84.1%, signifying that actual positive instances were correctly identified. These close values across all metrics suggest a consistent and balanced performance in predicting both positive and negative instances.

The adoption of the GradientBoostingClassifier model can significantly assist a human resource department dealing with high employee attrition by offering practical solutions for improving employee retention. By focusing on employee engagement, improving recruitment practices, and supporting management, HR departments can create an environment where employees feel valued and are less likely to leave.
